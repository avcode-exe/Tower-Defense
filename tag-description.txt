Release type: beta / pre-release.

This tag marks Tower Defense 1.5.1 Beta 1, a pre-release focused on the Necromancer monster, revive mechanics, no-split behavior, movement speed categories, dev-mode economy behavior, economy balance, split behavior, tests, validation, and packaging/publishing readiness.

Release summary:
- Added the Necromancer monster as a new enemy with revive behavior.
- Implemented revive mechanics that restore selected defeated monsters to a configured portion of health.
- Added no-split behavior support so selected monsters can be excluded from split chains.
- Defined movement speed categories and mapped existing and new monsters to those categories.
- Added dev-mode economy behavior where placement, upgrades, heals, and shields are free; `_addGold` keeps gold at `Infinity`; and shop cards remain visually affordable.
- Rebalanced economy values, including Healer cost and monster rewards.
- Preserved intended splitting behavior: Runner no-split, Brute splits into Grunts, Elite and Champion split one level lower, and revived monsters do not split.
- Validated behavior through the existing test suite and lint/format checks.
- Packaging and auto-update workflow remains tied to `npm run release`.

Detailed feature notes:

1. Necromancer monster

The Necromancer is introduced as a new monster type with its own identity, stats, and behavior. It is intended to create additional pressure by reviving defeated enemies rather than only relying on direct damage or bulk movement.

Necromancer values:
- Key: `Y`
- HP: `220`
- Speed: `0.8`
- Reward: `18`
- Leak: `2`
- Damage: `18`
- Attack speed: `1.0`
- Attack range: `1`
- `noSplit`: `true`
- Revive range: `2.0`
- Revive HP ratio: `0.5`
- Revive max targets: `5`
- Revive glow duration: `1.5`

The Necromancer is mapped to the slow movement speed category.

2. Revive mechanics

Revive mechanics allow the Necromancer to bring defeated monsters back into play under controlled limits. The revive behavior uses a defined range, target cap, and health ratio so the feature is impactful without becoming unbounded.

Revive configuration:
- Revive range: `2.0`
- Revive HP ratio: `0.5`
- Revive max targets: `5`
- Revive glow duration: `1.5`

Important behavior:
- Revived monsters are restored to a portion of health rather than full health.
- The number of revived targets is capped.
- Revived monsters do not split.
- Revive glow duration provides a visible indication of the revive effect.

3. Movement speed system

The movement speed system defines named categories with explicit numeric speeds. These categories are used to make monster pacing consistent and easier to tune.

Movement speed categories:
- Very slow: `0.6`
- Slow: `0.8`
- Medium: `1.0`
- Fast: `2.0`
- Very fast: `3.0`

Monster movement mapping:
- Grunt: medium
- Runner: very fast
- Brute: slow
- Elite: medium
- Champion: slow
- Boss: very slow
- Shielded: medium
- Spear: fast
- Necromancer: slow

4. Dev-mode economy

Dev-mode economy behavior is intended to make development and QA flows easier by removing economy friction during testing.

Dev mode behavior:
- Placement is free.
- Upgrades are free.
- Heals are free.
- Shields are free.
- `_addGold` keeps gold at `Infinity`.
- Shop cards are visually affordable.

This makes it possible to test tower placement, upgrades, monster waves, revive behavior, and economy-facing UI without manually managing gold.

5. Splitting behavior

Splitting behavior has been clarified and preserved for monsters that are intended to split, while selected monsters can opt out through `noSplit`.

Splitting rules:
- Runner has `noSplit`.
- Brute splits into Grunts.
- Elite splits one level lower.
- Champion splits one level lower.
- Revived monsters do not split.
- Necromancer has `noSplit: true`.

This prevents unintended recursive or repeated splitting from revived monsters and keeps no-split monsters from generating additional split targets.

6. Economy tuning

Economy tuning includes updates to monster rewards and tower costs to keep progression balanced around the new Necromancer and revive mechanics.

Economy values:
- Healer cost: `140`
- Monster reward is `base reward + 1`
- Necromancer reward: `18`

The reward formula ensures defeated monsters continue to provide predictable economy returns while allowing individual monster rewards to be tuned independently where needed.
